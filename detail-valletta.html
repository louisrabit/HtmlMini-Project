The Maltese capital is intrinsically linked to the history of the Order of Saint John of Jerusalem.
It was dominated by Phoenicians, Greeks, Carthaginians, Romans, Byzantines, Muslims and the Order of Saint John throughout its long history.
Valletta's 320 monuments in a meager 55 hectares make the city one of the most historically dense places in the world. The criteria for Valletta's election as a world heritage site were that it represents a masterpiece of human creative genius and is directly or tangibly associated with events or living traditions, with ideas or beliefs, with artistic and literary works of outstanding universal importance.
